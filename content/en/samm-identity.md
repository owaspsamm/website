## Who SAMM is

SAMM is a pragmatic community elder and steward of a shared body of knowledge. It focuses on improving the security of the software development lifecycle (SDLC), not judging the security of software products. Strong process leads to resilient outcomes over time.

## Voice and tone

SAMM speaks clearly and directly. Use simple language and active voice. Avoid jargon, hype, buzzwords, and marketing tone. Sound like a calm, experienced mentor. Be human. Be grounded.

Authority comes from observed patterns and real-world experience, not from institutional positioning. Avoid "industry best practice dictates." Prefer "Teams that skip this step often struggle with..."

## Maturity and scoring

Security maturity is layered. Organizations do not need to implement everything at once. Encourage starting where they are and improving what makes sense next.

Level 3 represents fully integrated and optimized practices. It is aspirational, not mandatory. Target maturity levels must be intentional and justified based on context, risk, and business goals. Scoring is directional, not judgmental.

Never shame. Never imply failure. Never compare organizations competitively. Avoid absolutes unless expressing core principles (e.g., SAMM is not punitive).

## Process focus

SAMM emphasizes development lifecycle practices. It focuses on improving process rather than proving product security. It reinforces long-term capability over short-term results.

## Learning curve

When content feels complex, explain it better. Provide rationale. Offer clear entry points. Reinforce that readers can start small.

## Community and collaboration

Open collaboration sits at SAMM's core. It works alongside industry and standards bodies. Humility is implied through evolution, not stated explicitly.

## Emotional contract

You are not late. You are not failing. You are somewhere. Let's improve deliberately.

## Content checklist

Use this before publishing documentation, talks, or messaging.

### Voice and tone

- Uses active voice
- Uses simple, direct language
- Avoids jargon and buzzwords
- Sounds like a mentor, not a vendor
- Avoids hype, urgency, or marketing language
- Avoids institutional stiffness

### Authority and posture

- Speaks from observed experience, not abstract "best practice"
- Does not sound defensive
- Does not perform humility
- Does not position SAMM against other frameworks or standards

### Maturity and scoring

- Frames maturity as progression, not judgment
- Encourages intentional target setting
- Avoids implying Level 3 is required
- Connects target levels to context, risk, and strategy
- When mentioning scores, includes next practical steps

### Process focus

- Emphasizes development lifecycle practices
- Focuses on improving process rather than proving product security
- Reinforces long-term capability over short-term results

### Learning curve

- Explains why structures exist
- Signals that readers can start small
- Offers clear entry points where possible
- Avoids overwhelming paragraph density
- Reduces intimidation without reducing rigor

### Emotional check

After reading, it should feel like:

- "This is layered. I can start small."
- "This makes sense."
- "I know what to do next."

If it instead feels like "We failed," "This is too much," or "This is abstract," rewrite.
